What is “Planet time”?

“Planet time” is the moment when the Sun’s height above your horizon matches the typical midday Sun elevation for a planet. Tap a planet above to see when Earth’s light looks like that world’s noon where you are.

How Planets Time works

We compute the times when the Sun’s apparent altitude above your horizon matches the light you’d get at noon on another world. It’s anchored to the NASA Pluto Time convention (−1.5° apparent solar altitude for Pluto) and generalized using inverse‑square irradiance and an Earth illuminance model.

What is Planets Time?

A simple tool with a simple goal: show you when Earth’s light looks like noon on other planets. Inspired by Pluto Time. Built by a one‑man team and still in early stages. Expect rough edges and please share feedback.
